Personality

Big Five personality characteristics represent the most widely used model for generally describing how a person engages with the world. The model includes five primary dimensions:
# Need Individuals who score high...
1 Agreeableness A person's tendency to be compassionate and cooperative toward others.
2 Conscientiousness A person's tendency to act in an organized or thoughtful way.
3 Extraversion A person's tendency to seek stimulation in the company of others.
4 Emotional range Is the extent to which a person's emotions are sensitive to the person's environment.
5 Openness Is the extent to which a person is open to experiencing different activities.

Needs

Needs describe at a high level those aspects of a product that are likely to resonate with the author of the input text. The following table describes the twelve needs that the service evaluates.
# Need Individuals who score high...
1 Excitement Want to get out there and live life, have upbeat emotions, and want to have fun.
2 Harmony Appreciate other people, their viewpoints, and their feelings.
3 Curiosity Have a desire to discover, find out, and grow.
4 Ideal Desire perfection and a sense of community.
5 Closeness Relish being connected to family and setting up a home.
6 Self-expression Enjoy discovering and asserting their own identities.
7 Liberty Have a desire for fashion and new things, as well as the need for escape.
8 Love Enjoy social contact, whether one-to-one or one-to-many. Any brand that is involved in bringing people together taps this need.
9 Practicality Have a desire to get the job done, a desire for skill and efficiency, which can include physical expression and experience.
10 Stability Seek equivalence in the physical world. They favor the sensible, the tried and tested.
11 Challenge Have an urge to achieve, to succeed, and to take on challenges.
12 Structure Exhibit groundedness and a desire to hold things together. They need things to be well organized and under control.

Values

Values describe motivating factors that influence the author's decision-making. The following table describes the five values that the service infers.
# Need Individuals who score high...
1 Self-transcendence / Helping others Show concern for the welfare and interests of others.
2 Conservation / Tradition Emphasize self-restriction, order, and resistance to change.
3 Hedonism / Taking pleasure in life Seek pleasure and sensuous gratification for themselves.
4 Self-enhancement / Achieving success Seek personal success for themselves.
5 Open to change / Excitement Emphasize independent action, thought, and feeling, as well as a readiness for new experiences.
